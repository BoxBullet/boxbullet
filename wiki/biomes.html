OVERVIEW

Biomes define world regions using Perlin-based noise generation with weighted thresholds, producing large-scale terrain diversity and rare extreme environments.

PRIMARY BIOME TYPES
Forest

Baseline land biome. Reduced dominance in recent updates to allow more biome diversity.

Meadow

Open grassland biome with lighter vegetation density and hedge generation support.

Desert

Rebalanced to match forest and meadow generation priority.

Ocean

Ultra low-frequency generation producing large contiguous water regions.

Beach

Transition biome generated along ocean borders for smooth coastline blending.

RARE & EXTREME BIOMES
Stony Peaks Rare

High-elevation extreme terrain generated from noise threshold extremes.

Brimstone Springs Rare

Volatile biome with orange-tinted overlay and high-contrast terrain visuals.

GENERATION RULES
  • Perlin noise drives biome distribution with adjusted Gaussian weighting.
  • Extreme noise values produce rare biome types more frequently.
  • Ocean generation uses ultra-low frequency noise (0.003f).
  • Beach biomes act as mandatory transition zones between land and ocean.
  • Rivers override standard land biomes during generation.
  • Oceans and beaches override river placement where conflicts occur.
BLENDING SYSTEM
  • Chunks blend biome colors using weighted neighbor sampling.
  • Radius 1 neighbors contribute strongest influence (0.85 weight).
  • Radius 2 and 3 neighbors provide diminishing smoothing effects.
  • Rivers are excluded from blending to preserve sharp water paths.
  • Brimstone biome applies an overlay tint above base terrain colors.
TECHNICAL NOTES
  • Chunk blending is amortized across frames using a processing queue.
  • Maximum of 500 blend operations per frame for performance stability.
  • Visible-only chunks are processed via frustum culling.
  • Heartbeat system refreshes stale or desynced biome data every 5 seconds.
