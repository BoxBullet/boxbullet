small_tree

Basic tree variant used primarily for low-density forest coverage. Serves as the baseline vegetation unit in most biomes.

large_tree

Expanded tree variant with increased height and footprint. Spawns less frequently and is used to create visual hierarchy in forest regions.

meadow_hedge

Decorative environmental structure used in meadow biomes. Adds ground-level density and improves biome readability without affecting traversal.

meadow_flowers

Small decorative flower clusters that spawn throughout meadow biomes. Designed to increase environmental variety and soften biome transitions.

stone

Generic environmental rock formation used across multiple biome types including forests, stony peaks, and brimstone regions.

tumbleweed

Lightweight desert vegetation object used to add motion and visual breakup to low-density desert terrain.

desert_log

Fallen dried log structure found within desert biomes. Used to increase environmental storytelling and terrain variation.

palm_tree

Tropical tree variant used in beach and desert-adjacent regions. Features a wider canopy and curved trunk profile.

beach_umbrella

Decorative beach prop used to improve shoreline visual identity and increase environmental diversity in coastal regions.

sailboat

Rare ocean structure that can generate in open water regions. Primarily intended as a long-range visual landmark.

sulfur_spring

Volcanic environmental feature found in Brimstone Springs biomes. Emits bright coloration and acts as a biome-identifying landmark.

Feature Generation System

BulletBox uses deterministic server-authoritative feature generation to ensure all connected players view identical environmental layouts.

  • Coordinate-based spatial hashing
  • Biome-specific feature pools
  • Server-synchronized placement
  • Adaptive feature scaling
  • Chunk-based feature streaming
